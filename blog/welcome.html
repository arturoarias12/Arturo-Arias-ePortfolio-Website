Hello, world: this site is live

A first entry on why I built this place, what it is for, and what I hope to do with it.

AA Arturo Arias May 21, 2026 3 min read

This is my first entry here, and it feels right to use it to explain what this place actually is.

I have wanted a proper personal website for a long time. The first version I built was years ago, using Django and Python. It worked, but it was more complex than it needed to be, and I eventually stopped maintaining it. This version is different: static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, no framework, no backend. It is purpose-built as an ePortfolio, and it is designed to live on GitHub Pages indefinitely without a running server. I used Claude Design (preview) as an aid throughout the process, which accelerated the work considerably and let me focus on decisions rather than boilerplate.

Why not just a résumé?

A résumé is, by nature, compressed. It lists outcomes, not process. It names tools, not reasoning. It has to fit on one or two pages, which means almost everything that makes the work interesting gets cut.

There is also a structural problem: résumés submitted online are frequently filtered by applicant tracking systems before a human ever reads them. That process rewards keyword density and formatting compliance, not the quality of the underlying work. This site is built for the reviewer who reaches it directly. The projects section covers BI and data engineering work, Python automation, machine learning experiments, and database systems, each with enough context to show the thinking behind it. A hiring manager or recruiter who wants to understand how I approach a problem can find the actual reasoning here, not just the outcome.

This site has more room. The projects section can show the actual approach, not just the result. The skills page can reflect real depth instead of a comma-separated list of keywords. And this blog is a place for things that do not belong anywhere else: research interests, observations from real work, reflections on topics like AI, data systems, communication, and the softer dimensions of technical roles.

There is also a practical side. A lot of my best work does not live on GitHub. Dashboards, client-facing reports, private analytics pipelines, case studies built inside company systems. None of that has a public URL. A portfolio like this gives that work a home, even if the full detail has to stay confidential.

What comes next

Some posts here will be long-form. Some will be brief field notes. Topics will range across data engineering, BI, AI, and the occasional piece on communication or team dynamics. I do not have a strict publishing schedule, but I expect to write regularly now that the infrastructure is in place.

If you are visiting because you found the link on LinkedIn or in a résumé: Welcome! I hope the experience is clean and the content is worth your time.

If something here sparked a thought or question, feel free to reach out.
