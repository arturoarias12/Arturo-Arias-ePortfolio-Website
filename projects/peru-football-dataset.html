Peru National Football Dataset

A curated, analysis-ready dataset covering Peru's national football team results from 1927 to 2024, including match details, venues, tournaments, altitude data, and coaching records, structured for statistical analysis and historical research.

Why this dataset?

Peru has a rich football history — Copa América campaigns, a World Cup appearance in 1982, and a highly vocal national following. But no single, clean, analysis-ready dataset covered the full sweep of the national team's results. Scattered match records existed across Wikipedia, football reference sites, and fan archives, each with different formats and coverage gaps.

The goal was to produce a structured CSV that any analyst could load and immediately begin exploring without data cleaning. Coverage starts from Peru's very first international match, played on November 1, 1927, against Uruguay.

Dataset structure

The main file is peru_match_results.csv. Each row represents one match and includes 21 columns across four thematic groups:

Field Description
Match result
match_idChronological match identifier (M001, M002, …)
dateMatch date
peru_score / rival_scoreGoals scored by each side
peru_awarded_score / rival_awarded_scoreScore after any post-match reviews or sanctions
resultW / D / L
shootout_resultPenalty shootout outcome, if applicable
awarded_resultResult after reviews or sanctions, if applicable
Opponent & competition
rivalOpposing national team
rival_confederationConfederation the rival belongs to (CONMEBOL, UEFA, etc.)
tournament_nameSpecific tournament name (e.g., FIFA World Cup 2018)
tournament_typeTournament category (e.g., FIFA World Cup, Copa América, Friendly)
officialBoolean — whether the match was an official FIFA fixture
Venue
stadiumName of the stadium
cityHost city
countryHost country
elevationCity elevation in meters above sea level
peru_conditionHome / Away / Neutral
Coaching
coachPeru head coach at time of match
coach_nationalityCoach's nationality

Three matches are intentionally excluded from the dataset: a 1988 fixture where Peru fielded a U-19 squad, a 2011 match where Senegal sent an alternative team, and a 2013 friendly against Basque Country, which is not recognized by FIFA.

Research use cases

  • Home advantage analysis controlling for altitude of venue
  • Win rate by coach era and tournament type
  • Performance trends over decades (qualification campaigns, Copa América record)
  • Opponent-specific historical head-to-head records
