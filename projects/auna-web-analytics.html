Two Power BI dashboards covering the full conversion funnel (sessions to leads to affiliations) across five healthcare websites for Auna and Oncosalud, powered by an automated three-source AWS pipeline that replaced a cumbersome daily manual process and reduced data processing time by 95%.

Context

Auna is a leading integrated healthcare provider operating in Peru, Colombia, and Mexico, with 31 healthcare facilities and over 1.3 million plan members. Its digital presence spans two distinct types of websites: e-commerce platforms where users purchase insurance plans, and healthcare service delivery websites for specialized medical programs.

Within the Growth Team (Digital Sales Area), the team monitored the e-commerce funnel across all these platforms: sessions (users visiting the site), leads (users leaving their contact information), and affiliations (users completing a purchase of an insurance plan or service). Leads are a required step before any affiliation, so the funnel is strictly sequential, and conversion rates at each stage are the key levers the team works to improve. Before this project, tracking those metrics required a daily manual routine: data was pulled from multiple separate sources, combined in a heavy Excel workbook, and uploaded to Google Sheets for stakeholder access. The process consumed significant analyst time every day and left little room for actual interpretation.

As a Data Analyst on the Growth Team, reporting to the Growth Leader, I was responsible for designing and delivering an automated solution: a unified data pipeline feeding two Power BI dashboards, giving the team always-current visibility into the full funnel without the manual overhead.

Scope

The initial deployment covered five websites across two categories:

  • E-commerce (insurance selling): Oncosalud (cancer insurance) and Auna Salud (health insurance)
  • Healthcare service delivery: Auna Maternidad (maternity care), Auna UMR (reproductive medicine), and Auna Segunda Opinión (second opinion consultations)

Following the initial launch, coverage expanded incrementally as additional websites in the portfolio were added.

What was built

Data pipeline

Three separate data sources had to be consolidated into one place: web session data (from Google Analytics 4), lead records, and affiliation data. Each lived in a different system with a different schema.

I built an initial pipeline using a Python notebook to extract, standardize, and load data from all three sources into AWS. I then co-worked with the data engineering team to productionize that pipeline into a robust end-to-end flow, ensuring reliable and scheduled data availability in AWS. Once all sources were centralized, I used Amazon Athena to query the data directly into Power BI, eliminating the need for any local exports or manual file handling in between.

This replaced the previous daily manual process entirely and reduced data processing time by 95%.

Data model and dashboards

I designed the full data model from scratch, using the existing Excel workbook as a functional reference for the metrics and breakdowns stakeholders were already accustomed to. Two Power BI dashboards were delivered, providing a structured view of the conversion funnel (sessions, leads, affiliations) segmented by platform, channel, and time period.

Two fellow Growth Data Analysts contributed domain expertise throughout the process, helping define what was most valuable to surface for analysis.

Note on data sensitivity

The data behind this project belongs to the organization it was built for. Dashboard screenshots and specific business metrics are not shared here out of respect for that. The content on this page describes the scope and approach at a high level.
